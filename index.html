Germany is Calling!

Dates, events and exactly when to do what

☀️ Summer in Germany is elite if you know where to look. Here are the dates that actually matter this month, plus the student admin windows most people miss completely.
🌸 Public Holidays and Key National Days +
June 21 🎵Fête de la Musique - free live music on every street in Berlin all day long. Do not miss this.
June 28 🏳️‍🌈Christopher Street Day (Pride Berlin) - one of Europe's biggest Pride events. Plan transport early.
July 19 🎶Lollapalooza Berlin at Olympiagelände - student tickets available. Check early bird deals now.
💡 Many Berlin state museums offer heavily discounted or free entry on national holidays. Always check before paying full price!
🎓 Student Admin Deadlines - Don't Miss These +
July 1 📝Winter semester applications open at most German universities. Some programmes fill within days.
July 15 🏠WG room listings spike - best time to search for an October room before supply runs out.
July 31 🎉Summer semester exams end at most universities. Results posted, Abschlussfeiern events start.
Aug 1 💼Werkstudent job postings PEAK. Companies plan their autumn headcount in August. Apply now.
📌 August is the single best month to apply for Werkstudent roles. Our research data confirms it - internationals who have field-related student jobs are 84% more likely to get hired full-time.

Student Discounts

Verified savings every student in Germany should know

Most students claim less than half the discounts they're entitled to in Germany. Here's your verified list for 2026 - fact-checked and ready to use 👇
🚇
Deutschland-Semesterticket 🔗
€34.80/month

The student version of the Deutschlandticket (€63/month standard in 2026). Bundled into your Semesterbeitrag at most German universities. Covers all local and regional transport nationwide.

🍽️
University Mensa
€2 - €4/meal

Studierendenwerk cafeterias. Hot meals, veggie and vegan options. Pay with your student card. Best affordable eating option in Germany, full stop.

🏛️
Museums and Culture
Up to FREE

Berlin Staatliche Museen: €5 with student ID vs €12 full price. Free entry on specific national holidays. Theatres and opera: always ask for student rates at the box office.

🎬
Cinema Nights
From €5

Cineplex, UCI, Yorck, Babylon, Sputnik - student prices from €5 to €7. Always show your student ID at the box office. Tuesday nights are often the cheapest.

📱
Spotify and Streaming 🔗
€5.99/month

Spotify Student, Apple Music Student, YouTube Premium Student. Verify at student.com using your university email or enrolment certificate.

✈️
ISIC Travel Card 🔗
From €15/year

International Student Identity Card. Discounts on flights, hostels, Interrail passes and car rentals across Europe. Pays for itself on one weekend trip.

🎯 Verified gem: Amazon Prime Student Germany - 6 months completely free, then €4.49/month (half the regular price). Sign up at amazon.de with your university email or Immatrikulationsbescheinigung.

What Changed in Germany

Visa, transport, jobs and benefits - June 2026 update

🛂
Urgent

Iranian Student Visa - Embassy Restrictions

German Embassy in Tehran has restricted operations. Thousands of admitted students cannot apply for visas. DEGIS published an official statement calling for urgent solutions through accessible third-country embassies. Share this with your university and International Office.

🚇
Price Update

Deutschlandticket Now €63/month (Student: €34.80)

From January 2026, the standard Deutschlandticket rose to €63/month. The student Deutschland-Semesterticket is approximately €34.80/month, bundled into your semester contribution at most universities. Check your university portal for your specific arrangement.

💰
Good News

BAföG Expanded - More International Students May Qualify

Germany expanded BAföG eligibility. International students with certain residence permits may now qualify for financial support. Check with your Studierendenwerk as soon as possible.

💼
Job Market

148,500 STEM Roles Still Unfilled in Germany

This Month's Insider Tip

The thing we wish someone had told us sooner

🌻 June Tip
Your Uni Library Card Unlocks Way More Than Books

Your university library card is not just for academic papers. Most German university libraries give access to: free e-books, digital newspapers including NY Times and FAZ, Babbel language learning, music databases, and in many cases discounted cinema tickets through Kulturticket partnerships. Most students never discover any of this.

🎬 In Berlin: Your student card gets you cinema tickets from €5 to €7 at Kino International, Babylon, Sputnik Kino and Eiszeit Kino. That's around 50% off for a proper night out.

📌 Mensa queue tip: eat before 11:30am or after 1:15pm. You will thank us. 😄

✊ DEGIS Statement
Support Iranian Students Facing Visa Barriers

Thousands of Iranian students admitted to German universities currently have no way to apply for their student visa due to restricted Embassy operations in Tehran. DEGIS calls for urgent temporary visa processing through German embassies in accessible third countries. This affects some of the most motivated international students in the pipeline.

What you can do: Share the statement with your university. Ask International Offices to extend admission validity deadlines. Raise this with the Federal Foreign Office.
